Student Activities

Undergraduate Student Board (USB)

USB is a student-run organization made up of twelve undergraduates selected from among the department's sophomores, juniors, and seniors. The goal of the USB is to improve the quality of the department as a whole by increasing the quantity and quality of the communication between students, faculty, and the department's corporate sponsors. USB aims to assist the faculty and administration by providing student feedback on courses and policies, and to assist students by ensuring that their voices are heard and respected.

Website: acm.cs.purdue.edu
Email: acm@cs.purdue.edu

Computer Science Women’s Network (CSWN)

CSWN is an organization made up of both students and staff who are dedicated to helping women in Computer Science. The leadership team that organizes most activities is made up of female students who want to reach out and help all women in CS. They organize different activities meant to encourage young women to meet one another and also learn more about their chosen field of study. Activities range from picnics and technical talks to helping students find tutors. In the past, they have held events such as a Scheduling Workshop, IBM Resume Workshop, Faculty Mixers, and end of semester dinners. You are encouraged to check out the CSWN website for information on upcoming events. Everyone is welcome to attend!

Website: acm.cs.purdue.edu
Email: acm@cs.purdue.edu

BoilerMake

The annual BoilerMake competition is a 36-hour hackathon where teams of students create software and hardware projects that solve important, real-world problems. BoilerMake brings together highly talented university students and representatives from top-tier companies to support the development of remarkable student projects. Participants travel to Purdue’s campus from institutions all across North America to spend the BoilerMake weekend showcasing their programming skills, and developing personal relationships with their peers.

Website: boilermake.org
Email: team@boilermake.org

Association for Computing Machinery (ACM)

ACM is one of the premier computer science college organizations existing to this day. The Purdue chapter invites CS majors to join and gain access to helpful resources, connect with fellow students, and attend various CS related events, such as Tech Talks with guest speakers from influential companies.

Website: acm.cs.purdue.edu
Email: acm@cs.purdue.edu
