Computer Science Bridge Program

Our mission in the Department of Computer Science is to prepare our best and brightest students for success in the field of computer science. In 2013, the Department of Computer Science established the first CS Bridge Program to support incoming students who may find themselves struggling in the first CS class (CS 180). During this two-week program in August, students make friendships and develop a network of peers that is vital to their academic success. The Bridge Program introduces students to basic computer science and programming concepts at a comfortable pace in a fun and informed environment.

Website: cs.purdue.edu/undergraduate/bridge/
Email: csbridge@purdue.edu

Grace Hopper

The Grace Hopper Celebration for Women in Computing is the world’s largest technical conference for women in computing. The conference was co-founded by Dr. Anita Borg and Dr. Telle Whitney in 1994 and is inspired by the legacy of Admiral Grace Murray Hopper. The Grace Hopper conference is designed to bring the research and career interests of women in computing to the forefront. Conference attendees have the chance to present recent achievements in research and to attend sessions which focus on female representation in today’s technology fields.
Purdue sent 27 undergraduate, graduate, and staff participants to the 2014 Grace Hopper conference in Phoenix, Arizona, largely thanks to the corporate sponsorships from Apple, ExactTarget, and Northrop Grumman. The 2015 Grace Hopper conference will be held October 14 – 16 in Houston, Texas.
