Outreach Programs

ROCS

The Computer Science Department and the College of Science at Purdue University support a unique program called Reaching Out for Computer Science (ROCS). ROCS is made up of computer science students with a passion for giving back to their community, particularly when it involves teaching about technology. The group works with students from kindergarten to 12th grade in the state of Indiana and surrounding areas.
In addition to doing information sessions, hands-on activities, and full computer science lessons, the ROCS group also is committed to helping develop new and exciting ways for students to get involved in the world of technology. If you are interested in becoming a part of ROCS, please contact Phil Sands (psands@purdue.edu).

Website: cs.purdue.edu/outreach/rocs.html
Email: psands@purdue.edu

Adventures in CS Summer Camp

Adventures in Computer Science Summer Camp Purdue University offers two annual summer camps, aimed at middle school and high school students respectively. The middle school camp is focused on introducing students to computational thinking and programming through a variety of tools including Scratch, App Inventor, Boe-bots, and JavaScript. The high school camp focuses on one of a variety of computer science "big ideas" using tools relevant to the field and centered around programming in Python. We welcome students from all areas and all levels of experience. For more information, please contact Phil Sands (psands@purdue.edu).

Website: cs.purdue.edu/outreach/adventures-in-cs.html
Email: psands@purdue.edu

MAGIC

The Computer Science Department and the College of Science at Purdue University have endorsed a special collaboration between university computer science students and after-school technology clubs for high school-aged girls. The Mentors for Aspiring Girls in Computing (MAGIC) program attempts to increase interest in technology career paths among young women through the creation of safe and positive learning environments, the use of relevant CS activities for instruction, and near-peer mentoring to encourage future success.
The MAGIC program currently addresses computer programming through a variety of activities including generative art, games, artificial intelligence, app development and computer security. This service learning group works with four Indiana high schools and serves about 40 students on a weekly or bi-weekly basis. If you are interested in learning more about the MAGIC program, or would like to learn more about how to get your school involved, please contact Phil Sands (psands@purdue.edu).

Website: cs.purdue.edu/outreach/magic.html
Email: psands@purdue.edu
