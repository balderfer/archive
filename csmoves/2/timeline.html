Timeline

  • 1962
  • The Department of Computer Science at Purdue University becomes the first computer science department in the United States.
  • 1963
  • The student chapter of the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) is started at Purdue.
  • The first master’s degree in Computer Science is awarded at Purdue.
  • 1966
  • The first PhD degree in Computer Science is awarded to Kenneth M. Brown.
  • 1967
  • The first bachelor’s degree in Computer Science is awarded at Purdue.
  • 1978
  • Purdue becomes the first university to install a VAX 11/780 computer system running UNIX. ASCII terminals go into faculty offices and e-mail is sent via uucp to ihnp4.
  • 1981
  • Over 500 new freshmen enter the Purdue Computer Science program.
  • The Purdue Computer Science Department is part of the original consortium of universities that create CSNET.
  • 1985
  • The Computer Science Department introduces the first supervised computing and teaching laboratories at Purdue.
  • 1989
  • The Computer Science K-12 Outreach Program is started at Purdue.
  • 1992
  • Purdue establishes a Chapter of Upsilon Pi Epsilon (UPE), the international Honor Society for the Computing Sciences.
  • 1995
  • The Computer Science Department starts the Corporate Partners Program and forms the Corporate Partners Council.
  • 1998
  • The Center for Education and Research in Information Assurance and Security (CERIAS) is formed at Purdue from the COAST lab and is one of the world’s leading centers for research and education in areas of information security.
  • The first Computer Science Department Job Fair is held.
  • 1999
  • The Undergraduate Student Board (USB) is founded.
  • 2001
  • The Computer Science Women’s Network (CSWN) is created.
  • 2002
  • The Graduate Student Board (GSB) is created.
  • Computer Science faculty create software security company Arxan Technologies.
  • 2006
  • The Computer Science Department moves to the Lawson Computer Science Building named for Richard and Patricia Lawson.
  • 2010
  • The Center for Science of Information is established at Purdue as an NSF Science and Technology Center.
  • 2014
  • The Computer Science Department hosts its first “BoilerMake” hackathon competition; and over 400 undergraduate programmers from 19 colleges and universities across the U.S. and Canada participate in the 36-hour hackathon on Purdue’s campus.
  • 316 new freshmen enter the Purdue Computer Science program, and the program’s enrollment of undergraduate students surpasses 1,000 for the first time.
