    Marvin L. Hackman joined the firm in 1961, after serving as law clerk to the Chief Judge of the United States District Court in Indianapolis. His primary areas of practice have been representing developers, owners, and tenants of commercial real estate, including shopping centers, office buildings, and hotels; banks and other financial institutions making loans secured by real estate and security interests in personal property; and small business organizations, including equity offerings and other types of financing. Mr. Hackman has also represented clients in other areas such as buy-sell agreements, employment contracts and non-compete agreements.

    Mr. Hackman is a graduate of St. Joseph’s College (AB summa cum laude) and Indiana University School of Law (JD with high distinction). He also served as editor-in-chief of the Indiana Law Journal. He is a member of the Indianapolis Bar Association, the Indiana State Bar Association, and the Real Property, Probate and Trust Law Section of the American Bar Association. He has served as a director of several civic associations.
